HOST CLUB COME FROM BEHIND TO RETAIN TROPHY

 

March 26, 2006

 

Host and defending champions Tornadoes Swim Club, without their top swimmer Brad Hamilton, came from behind in the final session of the two-day Tornadoes Invitational Swim Meet to retain the championship trophy with 1,104.50 points.  Archrivals Y-Speedos with 958.50 points finished second.  The Y-Speedos, led by CARIFTA representative, Gillian Millwood, dominated the championships for the first two (2) sessions, but the host club never gave up and put on some sterling performances in the relays and 50M sprints to claim victory.

 

A total of fourteen  (14) records were broken.  Gillian Millwood (17) accounted for two (2) Meet Records.  In the Girls 11 and Over 200M Butterfly, she broke her own record of 2:46.10 to set a new Meet record of 2:41.92.  She then went on to capture the Girls 11 and Over 400M Individual Medley in a new Meet Record of 6:03.27 seconds erasing the old record of 6:03.51 seconds.  Other record holders were:

 

Breanna Roman (10)           Girls 10 and Under 200M Individual Medley

                                        Girls 10 and Under 100M Freestyle

 

Antoine Young (9)              Boys 10 and Under 200M Individual Medley

                                        Boys 10 and Under 100M Freestyle

 

David Bromfield (12)          Boys 11-12 200M Backstroke

 

Robert Marshall (8)            Boys 8 and Under 50M Breaststroke

 

Dominic Lee   (19)             Boys 11 and Over 400M Individual Medley

 

In the Masters Section:

 

          Janique Ricketts                 Female 25  34 50M Butterfly

                                                  Female 25  34 50M Freestyle

 

          Latrouvre Foster                Male 25  34 50M Butterfly

Ewan Allen                        Male 25  34 50M Backstroke

 

Also erasing the record of the Girls 10 and Under 200M Individual Medley were Octivia Gray and Stefani Webley, and Sidrell Williams in the Boys equivalent.  Andrew Williams, swimming in the Masters category, also erased the record in the Male 25-34 50M Backstroke.

 

Completing the top five (5) were:  Blue Seals - 521 points, Electric Eels  380 points and Marlins  198 points.
