ALIA ATKINSON QUALIFIES FOR 100 BREAST SEMI-FINAL – HAMILTON BREAKS JAMAICA’S 100 M BUTTERFLY AGE GROUP RECORD

 

March 19, 2006

 

Alia Atkinson this morning swam 1:15.16 in the Women’s 100 M Breaststroke to qualify for this evening’s semi-final at the Commonwealth Games. Atkinson swam in lane one of heat two had a great start (.75 reaction time) and pull out reaching the 15 M mark second. Her split at the 50 M was 35.15. St. Lucia’s Danielle Beaubrun also moved to the next round with the sixteenth fastest time (1:17.96).

 

Brad Hamilton lowered Jamaica’s 15-17 National Age Group 100 M Butterfly Record swimming a fast 58.21. The previous record of 58.58 was set by Ramon James swimming for Jamaica at the 2000 Caribbean Island Swimming Championships held in Aruba. With his fastest start in the Game (.68), Hamilton had a 50 M split of 27.08. Surprising the field, the lead swimming going into the semi-finals is Ryan Pini of Pappa New Guinea who swam 53.50. Bahamian Jeremy Knowles is qualified eighth with a time of 54.42.

 

Alia Atkinson and Tamara Swaby both swam the Women’s 50 M Freestyle in Heat four. Atkinson finished 7th (28.04) and Swaby 8th (28.14) and an overall ranking of 23rd and 24th respectively.

 

The Men’s 400 M Individual Medley had four swimmers form the Caribbean and three have qualified for the final this evening seeded 6th, 7th and 8th. Bradley Ally (Barbados) swam 4:24.94 to finish third in his heat. Jeremy Knowles was third in 4:27.40. Shaune Fraser had finished 4th in heat one to take the final place in the final swimming 4:29.26. Scotland’s Euan Dale leads the qualifiers with a time if 4:21.88. Cayman’s Andrew MacKay swam 4:51.76 to finish 11th.
