LEE BREAKS 13-14 100 M FREESTYLE AGE GROUP RECORD

Dominic Lee, with another record-breaking performance, set the pace for another good session for the Jamaicans at the Central American and Caribbean Swimming Championships (CCCAN) in Santo Domingo leading seven swimmers into this evenings finals.

Qualifying for the final in 6th position, Lee swam a personal best 56.45 to break Kevin Thompson’s 13-14 100 M Freestyle National Age Group Record of 56.71. In the same event, Todd Johnson swam a personal best 57.86 finishing 14th overall.

In the 15-17 Male 100 M Freestyle, Andrew Saunders missed the Andrew Phillips 22 years old Age Group Record by just .05 second, swimming 54.95 qualifying for the final with teammate Jevon Atkinson who timed 55.11.

Allison Bardowell made the Female 13-14 200 M Backstroke final in 8th place with a time of 2:39.54. In the next event, Todd Johnson knocked 2 seconds off his personal best time 200 M Backstroke time swimming 2:30.30, but failed to make the final.

Ramon James qualified for the 18 and Over 100 M Butterfly final with the 7th fastest time, when he swam 58.53.

Yesterdays record breaker Alia Atkinson is second alternate in the 11-12 100 M Breaststroke with a time of 1:24.00. In the 11-12 100 Butterfly, Brad Hamilton swam a personal best 1:13.37 (14th) behind Travis Forte who swam 1:10.70 (11th).

The finals start at 5:00 PM (4:00 PM Jamaica time).
