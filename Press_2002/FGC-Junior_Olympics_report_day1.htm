March 17, 2002

On Day 1 of the Florida Gold Coast Short Course Junior Olympics the Jamaicans on the relays failed to medal but other Caribbean swimmers picked up 3.

Chris Vythoulkas of Bahamas anchored the FLST A open 200 free relay in 21.04(24.18) to the gold in a time of 1:26.57.Bradley Ally of Barbados swam a 22.38 (25.72) to help the FLST B team to 5th in 1:29.43. Jevon Atkinson of Comets led off their team in 22.66 (26.05) for a 13th placing in 1:35.01.

In the open 800 free relay Chris leadoff FLST A in 1:42.59 (1:57.25) to capture the gold in 6:56.08 . Bradley's 1:45.51 (2:00.58) propelled the B team to the silver in 7:03.61 . A solid lead leg by  Jevon   1:50.98 (2:06.83) helped Comets to their highest placing of the day 10 th in 7:43.37.

For the girls Alia Atkinson and Nicky Cain did double duty swimming both  the 13-14 relays and the open relays . In the 13-14 200 free  relay Alia swam a swift 2nd leg 25.20 (28.63) on the FLST A team for 7th in 1:47.37. this was not enough  however to stop  compatriot Nicky's Comets from finishing ahead of them 5th with her 2nd leg split of 26.05 (29.60) in 1:46.63.  Unfortunately in the 800 free relay Alia's team was DQ'd.

In the open relays Nicky 26.43 (30.03) swam the anchor to help Comets B to 9th in 1:44.32. Allison Bardowell led off FLST A in 25.97(29.51) for 11th in 1:44.80. They were followed  by Flst B with Melissa Bardowell 2nd leg and Alia anchoring showing true sprint Qualities repeating the 25.20 (28.63) she swam previously.

The 800 free relay saw another round of fast swimming .Allison anchor split 1:56.95 (2:12.14) for 6th place for Flst A 8:04.15 .In 10th place Melissa split 2:03.15 (2:20.24) and Alia 2:03.15 (2:19.15) to aid FLST B in doing a total time of 8:17.53.

Tony Morrison
