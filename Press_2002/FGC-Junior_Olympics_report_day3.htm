March 20, 2002

The buildup to CARIFTA continues to be intense as the Jamaicans continue to record personal best times. Despite this, only 2 medals were garnered by our swimmers through the Atkinson siblings Alia and Adi. For the rest of the Caribbean contingent it continued to be a Bajan and Bahamian as Chris and Bradley accounted for 5 Golds and a Bronze.

Alia accounted for the lone individual medal as she blasted her prelims time of 25.36 (28.82) to grab the silver in a fast 24.94 (28.34) the fastest time by a Jamaican girl in the day. Adi with a 1:00.04( 1:09.41) breast leg combined with Bradley's back 53.26 (1:01.22) to grab the Bronze in 3:33.96 in the open 400 medley relay. Bradley took the 500 free in 4;40.20 (4:13.57) and  the 100 back 52.17 (59.93). Chris got his victories in the 50 free 21.56 (24.78), the 100 back 51.10 (58.76) for a total time of 3:28.31.

Other good performances by event finalists Allison in the(6th) 15-16 100 back 1:01.76 (1:09.79)  Raine(5th) in the 15-16 200  breast 2:31.78 (2:52.48), Adi (4th) 15-16 200 breast  2:14.85 (2;35.90), Jevon (9th)  open100 back 55.85 (1:04.21) Andrei Cross (Bar)(10th) open 50 free 22.45 (25.80), Nicky Cain (8th)13-14 100 back 1:04.51(1;12.57)
