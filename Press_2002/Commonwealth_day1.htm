JAMAICAN SWIMMERS DIVE INTO ACTION AT THE 2002 COMMONWEALTH GAMES

Home

July 30, 2002 ...Janelle Atkinson kicked off Jamaica’s 2002 Commonwealth Games campaign with a 9th place finish in the 200 M Freestyle while Angela Chuck swam a season best to finish 19th. Ramon James and Tamara Swaby both failed to qualify for the semifinals but swam season best times in the 50 M Back and 50 M Butterfly respectively.

In the 200 M Free, Atkinson who is better known as a distance swimmer, got off to a good start and remained with the leaders throughout the race. At the 50 m mark, Atkinson touched 5th in 28.99 but pulled herself into 4th by the halfway mark. With 50 metres to go, she had passed Zimbabwe’s Mandy Leach to be third and made the turn in 1:31.14. The last 50 metres saw the top three swimmers fighting hard for a qualifying spot in the finals but at the finish Atkinson just missed the final spot touching in 2:02.18, just 1 second behind England’s Karen Gregg who touched in 2:01.18 and Australia’s Giaan Rooney (2:01.73). Atkinson is the 1st alternate for the final.

The next qualifying heat, which proved to be the fastest of the event, found Jamaica’s Angela Chuck was up against top seed Elka Graham from Australia. In was England’s Karen Pickering, who fed on the noisy home crowd who stole the Australian’s thunder to finish with the fastest preliminary time of 2:00.89. Chuck touched 7th with a season best time of 2:09.20.

Ramon James got off to a good start in the 50 Meter Backstroke surfacing just before the 15 metre mark with Australia’s Ethan Rolff. With less than 2 seconds separating the top seven swimmers, James timed 27.99 to finished 6th in the heat and 19th overall. His time was just .53 sec slower than the 16th place semifinal qualifying swim.

In a very competitive 50 metre Butterfly event, Tamara Swaby missed qualifying for the semifinals by .8 sec. Swaby who is better known for swimming the longer distance Butterfly  event started slowly but got into her rhythm buy the 25 meter mark. She started gaining on the leaders, she ran out off pool and touched in 29.28 to finish 22nd overall.

One other notable Caribbean result was Jeremy Knowles making the finals of the 200 M Butterfly. Swimming in heat two, Knowles found himself up against two Australian’s Heath Ramsey and Grant McGregor. Half way through the race, Knowles found himself in 4th but swam a fantastic final 100 metres to take the lead just before the final turn. In the last 50 metres, refused to relinquish his lead, touching in 2:00.31, the fourth fastest time going into the final.

Mariana Chuck will dive into action tomorrow when she swims the 100 M Backstroke. Ramon James will swim the 50 M Butterfly while Angela Chuck will compete in the 100 M Freestyle.
