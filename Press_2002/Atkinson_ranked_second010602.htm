JANELLE ATKINSON SECOND IN WORLD RANKINGS  

Home

January 6, 2002 - Jamaica’s swimming sensation Janelle Atkinson is now ranked second in the World in the SwimNews Short Course 800 Metres Freestyle rankings. Atkinson’s time of 8:20.13, swum at the United States Open on November was second only to China’s Hua Chen, who leads the rankings with a time of 8:15.15.  Atkinson’s time at the US Open (8:20.13) also set a new Commonwealth Record. Her US Open times in the 400 M Free (4:05.39) and 200 M Free (1:59.15) are ranked and impressive 7th and 24th respectively.

With Atkinson’s sights firmly set on the Commonwealth Games this summer, her competition in the 400 and 800 M Freestyle are becoming apparent as three Commonwealth swimmers are interestingly positioned within 2 seconds of each other in the rankings. In the 800 M Freestyle, Australia’s Amanda Pascoe and Great Britain’s Rebecca Cook will look to challenge the Commonwealth record holder, Atkinson. Pascoe is ranked third (8:21.27), just 1.14 seconds behind Atkinson and Cook fourth with a time of 8:21.47.

The 400 M Freestyle is shaping up to be just as exciting as Amanda Pascoe presently has the edge with her time of 4:05.28 (ranked 6th), just ahead of Atkinson (4:05.39) and another British girl, Nicole Jackson who swam 4:05.49 (ranked 8th) in Australia last August.

Janelle Atkinson is presently attending and competing for the University of Florida. While she is participating in regular collegiate competitions, her next major challenge will be the NCAA Nationals in April 2002.
