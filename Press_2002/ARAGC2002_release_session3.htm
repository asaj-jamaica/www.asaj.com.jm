timothy wong smashes andrew phillip's 23 year old record at age group swimming championships

Home

March 2, 2002

While the Y-Speedos (895) widened their point’s lead over the Tornadoes Swim Club (815), the Tornadoes stole the show by breaking three Age Group Championship Meet Records. Timothy Wong (Tornadoes Swim Club) shattered Andrew Phillip’s 23 year old 15 & Over 400 M Freestyle meet record of 4:27.70, swimming 4:24.94 to take the Gold ahead of his team mate Matthew Stewart (4:39.86).

Just four events before, Stewart had lost his 11-12 400 M Freestyle Record to Brad Hamilton (Tornadoes) who swam a fast 4:57.92 knocking a whopping 14 seconds off the old record of 5:08.47. Hamilton and Y-Speedos Alford Green raced neck and neck until the 200 M mark, turning just .23 seconds apart. Hamilton then turn up the pace and pulled away to win the Gold as Green finished in a very respectable time of 5:00.46.

Jason Lindo’s 15 and Over 200 M Breaststroke record of 2:44.75 set in 1990 seemed to be untouchable but Dominic Lee made no doubt as he lowered it by 6 seconds touching in 2:38.69, a full 10 second ahead of the improving Sheridan Chung (Tornadoes) and Jason James (Y-Speedos) who swam 2:49.94.

The Marlins Swim Club with some excellent swims in the * and Under and 9-10 age groups have moved into third place (120), pushing the Flying Fish Ambassadors into forth (96).

Session 4 of the Championships starts at 4:30 this afternoon at the National Stadium Pool.
