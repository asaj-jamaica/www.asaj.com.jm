AFTER SEVENTY FINALS ‘Y’ SPEEDOS ARE STILL IN THE LEAD IN THE KARL DALHOUSE INTERNATIONAL MEMORIAL SWIM MEET

Home

January, 26, 2002

Five more records fell today at the ‘Y’ Speedos’ Karl Dalhouse Memorial Swim Meet. Setting the pace with two records each were, Speedos’ Ramon Walton and Tornadoes Dominic Lee. Ramon Walton erased the old mark of 38.50 in the 9-10 Boys 50m Backstroke in 37.79, and in the 200m Individual Medley swam 2:58.37 to better the old record of 2:58.53. Dominic Lee of Tornadoes smashed the 13 & Over Boys 200m Individual Medley of 2:24.66 in a time of 2:19.28, as well as the 200m Freestyle record of 2:0571 in a new time of 2:05.63. Todd Johnson of Speedos was the day’s other record breaker in the 15 & Over Backstroke bettering the old record of 1:05.99 in 1:04.26.

The overseas swimmers from the Dorados de Paraiso Swim Club of the Dominican Republic and the Stingrays Swim Club of Cayman  have been giving stiff competition for the local girls, through sister Isabella and Maria Zenoni and Andrea Balderamus, who, among them have so far won six (6) events and placed in four others.

Sunday morning’s final session promises to provide more exciting swimming as the Clubs continue to battle for top honours.

At the close of today’s events ‘Y’ Speedos were in the lead with 969 points over Tornadoes with 832 points. In third place was the Marlins Swim Club on 255 points closely followed by Stingrays on 252 points.

Barbara Briggs, Meet Co-ordinator.
