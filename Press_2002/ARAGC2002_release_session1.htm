TORNADOES SWIM CLUB LEADS AGE GROUP SWIM CHAMPS AFTER DAY ONE

Home

February 28, 2002

After ten (10) finals of the Walter Rogers Age Group Swimming Championships, the Tornadoes Swim Club has scored 147 points to edge ahead of the Y-Speedos Swim Club who have scored 133 and the Flying Fish Ambassadors 5.

Y-Speedos Tiffany Walton started the meet on a good note by winning two events both with personal best times. Walton knocked 12 seconds off her previous best 800 M Freestyle time swimming 10:31.07 to win the event ahead of Tornadoes Rene Wright (10:33.19) and Y-Speedos Kristie Irving (10:48.29), both personal best times.

Walton returned to the pool 60 minutes later to take the Gold in the 13-14 200 M Individual Medley in an exciting finish with Gillian Millwood. Walton led by over a body length at the 150 M mark but Millwood finished strong and missed the Gold by just .23 of a second as Walton touched in 2:51.07. Millwood swam 2:51.30 to take the Silver and Kellye-Jean Barnaby third in 3:00.08, all personal best times.

Dominic Lee was just .01 second off Sean Brinn’s 11 year old 15.& Over 200 M Individual Medley meet record swimming 2:21.73 to win the event ahead of Y-Speedos James Smith (2:28.64) and Tornadoes Sheridan Chung (2:20.39 PB).

Tornadoes Gillian Stewart (2:49.18) and Brad Hamilton (2:37.08) swam personal best times to win their respective 11-12 200 M Individual Medley events. Both times were CARIFTA Qualifying times.

For more information and the full results for session1, go to www.swimjamaica.com. The competition continues at the National Stadium Pool on Friday at 5:30.
