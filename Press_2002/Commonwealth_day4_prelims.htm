ATKINSON READY FOR 800 M FREE FINAL AND MARIANA CHUCK AND RAMON JAMES ADVANCE TO SEMIFINALS

Home

August 2, 2002....With Mariana Chuck and Ramon James both advancing to the semifinals and three of the four swim being best times, Jamaica had another good session in the pool at the Commonwealth Games being held in Manchester England.

In the Women’s 50 M Backstroke, Mariana Chuck swam a personal best 32.42, placing 15th and securing a spot in the semifinals. Chuck got off to a slow start, surfacing last some three metres behind the leader but quickly picked up her pace to finish 7th in the heat. Australia’s Diana Calub won the heat in 29.33 while England’s Sarah Price (29.31) has the fastest time going into the semifinal.

Swimming in the same heat as Australia’s Ian Thorpe, Ramon James swam 1:00.29 to qualify for the Men’s 100 M Backstroke semifinals to be swum this evening. Thorpe won the heat in 56.45 while his teammate Matt Welsh, who was disqualified for a false start in the 200 M Backstroke yesterday, is the top semifinal seed with a time of 56.35.

The Women’s 50 M Freestyle saw Lisa Curry-Kenny’s (New Zealand) Commonwealth Games record (25.80) being smashed twice in successive heats. Alice Mills (Australia) swimming in heat 4 was the first to crack the record when she swam 25.65. In the next heat Alison Sheppard (Scotland) and Toni Jeffs (Australia) both went faster than the new record with Sheppard winning in a fast 24.68, just .55 seconds off Inge de Bruign World Record (24.13). Jeffs was second to touch in 25.43. Angela Chuck (27.09) and Tamara Swaby (27.25) both failed to advance but swam personal best times with Chuck touching just .01 off the Jamaican National Record. Chuck finished 22nd and Swaby 25th.

Jamaica will therefore have three swimmers in the today’s finals session scheduled to start at 7:00 PM local time as a relaxed Janelle Atkinson is set to compete in the final of the 800 M Freestyle.
