March 18, 2002

It was an improved performance for Jamaica with continued excellent performances by other regional swimmers. Alia , Allison , Chris and Bradley were the pick of the West Indian swimmers.

In 200free Alia fell down from her Prelims seeding but bettered her time to 2:00.42 (2:16.07) for 7th.In the 100 fly it was not to be same story as she moved from a 7th seeding to a silver medal in a speedy 1:01.07 (1:08.62) a silver medal time at last year's CARIFTA.

Allison also   proved herself ready for the regional champs by blasting through to a 1:56.05(2:11.13) a gold medal time for her age group at last year's games. She then added to the medal collection  by capturing the bronze in the 100 fly just nipped in the death of the race by Plantation's Katherine Noland 59.28(1:06.60) to 59.56 (1:06.92).

Jevon registered a big time drop in the 200 back by stopping the clock in 1:59.22 (2:17.03) for 4th, a time that could land him in the medals at CARIFTA.

Chris of Bahamas added to his treasure trove by taking 2nd in the open 200 free in 1:41.59 (1:56.10) with Bajan Andrei Cross 3rd in 1:42.73 (1:57.40) he then mined gold in the 100 fly 49.66 (56.11)  and a 24.60 (28.270 back lead off to get his 2nd gold of the day in the 200medley relay in a top 16 time of 1:36.35.       

Bradley continued the gold rush for FLST by taking the 200 back in 1:52.43 (2:09.22) and the 100 fly 52.01 (58.76). He then combined with Adi Atkinson to get the bronze in the 200 medley relay in 1:37.69 another top 16 time. Bradley back 25.02 (28.76) and Adi breast 26.98(31.19).
