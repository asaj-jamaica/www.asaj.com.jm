‘Y’ SPEEDOS TAKE THE LEAD, AS FOUR RECORDS FALL IN THE KARL DALHOUSE MEMORIAL INTERNATIONAL SWIM MEET

Home

January, 25, 2002

The ‘Y’ Speedos are in the lead with 366 points  after 22 events in the first day’s swimming of the Karl Dalhouse Memorial Meet. In an evening of exciting swimming in the 9-10 girls and boys events there were some close finishes. In the 100m freestyle Girls,  Speedos’ Taryn Bridgewater narrowly beat Marlins’ Giselle Hall in 1:16.65 to 1:16.96, and the ‘Y’ Speedos 9-10 Girls 200m Freestyle relay team edged out Marlins in a time of 2:25.26 to 2:25.30.

The four meet records to fall were by Tornadoes’ Tamari Farquharson in the 11-12 Girls 100m Breaststroke in 1:12.94,to the old record 1:26.82; Maria Zenoni from the Dorados de Paraiso Swim Club (Domincan Republic) in the 15 & over 100m Breaststroke beating the old record of 1:23.90 in 1:23.59;  Tornadoes’ Dominic Lee in the 15 & over Boys 100m Breaststroke in a time of 1:10.34 to the old record of 1:13.66 and the ‘Y’ Speedos’ 9-10 Boys 200m Freestyle relay team of Ramon Walton, Dane Todd, Clive Green and Donovan Todd in 2:15.70 (old record2:17.51)

The points standing at the end of the day were:

          ‘Y’ Speedos               366

          Tornadoes                  318

          Marlins                        96

          Stingray of Cayman     69

          Royals                        53

          Blue Seals                  42

          Dorados de Paraiso     27

          Aqua Youth                 7

          Montego Bay All Stars  5     

          Unattached Dolphins     3

The meet continues tomorrow at the National Stadium Pool at 8.30 o’clock in the morning and 4.00 o’clock in the afternoon with what promises to be more exciting swimming in store.

Barbara Briggs, Meet Co-ordinator
