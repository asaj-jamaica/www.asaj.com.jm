A Mixed Day for Jamaica on Day 2 of the Commonwealth Games 2002

Home

July 31, 2002 ...Jamaica had a mixed day at the XVII Commonwealth Games Swimming Competition as Ramon James swam a personal best time in the 50 M Butterfly, while Angela Chuck and Mariana Chuck were just off their best times in the 100 Freestyle and 100 Backstyle respectively. Both of the Chuck girls just missed qualifying for the semifinals.

In the first event of the session, Ramon James swam a personal best time of 26.70 in the 50 M Butterfly preliminaries. Despite finishing third in his heat, James placed 27th overall and did not advance. Australia’s Geoff Huegill leads the fastest 16 swimmers into the semifinals after setting the Commonwealth Games record of 24.10.

Swimming in her first major international competition, a very nervous Mariana Chuck started the 100 M Backstroke hoping to improve her time and qualify for the evening’s semifinals. She went out fast and turned at the 50 metres in 33.10, just a body length behind leader Sarah Price (Australia). After the turn, Chuck tired fast and fell behind the leaders, finishing seventh in 1:09.65, just off her personal best 1:09.63. Chuck will be the first alternate for the semifinals, which will be held this evening. Price won the heat and is the top seed going into the semifinals with a new Commonwealth Games record time of 1:01.16.

Angela Chuck was just .13 second off her personal best time in the 100 M Freestyle swimming 58.44 to place 19th overall. Chuck left the blocks with the 4th fastest reaction time (.74 sec) in the event and looked strong at the end of the first lap, turning 5th in 27.68. With 15 M to go, Leah Martingdale (Barbados) and Yeo Joscelin (Singapore), who had trailed Chuck to that point, eased past to push her into 7th place. Australia’s Jodie Henry swam the fastest time in the heats, swimming 55.79. The semifinals will be held this evening.

Two Jamaican’s are scheduled to compete in preliminary events tomorrow morning. Janelle Atkinson will compete in the 800 M heats and should advance to the final. If she can swim at her best, Atkinson is fancied to be in the medal hunt when the final is swum on Friday evening. If the seed times are anything to go by, Tamara Swaby will have to lower her personal best time swim of 1:04.97 to advance to the finals of the 100 M Butterfly. She clearly has the potential to do so if she maintains the form shown in the 50 M Butterfly when she swam under 29.28 sec.
