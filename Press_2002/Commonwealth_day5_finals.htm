JANELLE ATKINSON WINS SECOND COMMONWEALTH GAMES BRONZE MEDAL. RELAY TEAM BREAKS NATIONAL RECORD

Home

August 3, 2002…..Janelle Atkinson claimed Jamaica’s second Commonwealth Games swimming medal when she won the Bronze in the 400 M Freestyle. Just minutes later, the 4 X 100 Freestyle Relay team of Atkinson, Tamara Swaby, Mariana Chuck and Angela Chuck smashed the National Record by eight seconds.

One day after winning her first Commonwealth Games medal, a Bronze in the 800 M Free, Atkinson climbed on the starting blocks for her favorite event, the 400 M Free. Seeded in lane two, Atkinson started with another excelent reaction time (.67 seconds) and took the early lead. By 25 metres, however, Australia’s Elke Graham had edged in to the lead and after the first turn turn she was followed by England’s Rebecca Cooke and together slowed opened up a lead that Atkinson could not close in her last surge.

With 60 metres to go, and in 5th place, Atkinson picked up the pace and passed Canada’s Jessica Deglau just before the 350 meter turn. Heading for the finish, Atkinson slowly closed in on the leaders and with 10 metres from the finish, eased passed a fading Amanda Pascoe (Australia) to claim the Bronze. Rebecca Cooke touched first to stop the clock at 4:09.49, Graham second in 4:11.47 and Atkinson third timing 4:13.24.

In the last event of the evening, Jamaica’s Women’s 4 X 100 Freestyle relay team smashed the National Record of 4:04.76. Atkinson swam the first leg swimming a fast 57.70 and handed over to Tamara Swaby who despite coming down with the flu, swam a respectable 58.80 second leg. Mariana Chuck then swam the third leg in a spectacular 1:01.34 split time, well under her best 100 M Free time of 1:05.91. Angela Chuck took over to swim the anchor leg and battled to close the gap on Wales who was just three seconds ahead. Wales held on and Chuck touched sixth in a new Jamaican National Record time of 3:56.54, a full eight seconds faster than the old record set in 1999 at the Pan American Games.

Swaby’s illness has forced her to scratch from the 200 M Butterfly scheduled for tomorrow. A decision will be made on the 4 X 100 Medley Relay in the morning.

If both events are scratched, the Jamaicans will have concluded a very successful competition, reaping two Bronze Medals, placing two others into semifinals and establishing two new National Records. The swimming competition will end tomorrow afternoon, immediately followed by the Games closing ceremony.  Janelle Atkinson, Tamara Swaby, Ramon James will return to Jamaica on August 5th while the Chuck sisters will return on the 7th.
