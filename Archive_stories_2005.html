DECEMBER 1, 2005 - A new ASAJ Executive was last night elected at the Associations Annual General Meeting. John Eyre was returned unopposed as President as was Locksley Todd (1st Vice President) and Garth Henriques (Vice President Water Polo). Michelle McIntyre is the new Vice President Synchronized Swimming and Faith Palmer the new Secretary. Kendis Nangle will remain in the Position of Vice President but will demit office as soon as a suitable replacement is found. The positions of Treasurer and Assistant Secretary/Treasurer remain open and will be appointed by Council.

SEPTEMBER

September 30, 2005 - Jamaica's National Junior Men's Water Polo Team will leave the island tomorrow for Fort Lauderdale enroute to Argentina where they will compete in the 13th FINA Men's Junior Water Polo World Championships. The team will play a number of practice matches at the Fort Lauderdale Hall of Fame Pool before leaving for Mar del Plata, Argentina on Monday October 3rd.

September 30, 2005 - Jamaica's Water Polo Referee Mark Jones left the island today for Argentina to participate in a Water Polo Referee's School prior to officiating in the 13th FINA Men's Junior Water Polo World Championships (October 6-15)

September 15, 2005 -Jamaica's Minister of Sport, the Hon. Portia Simpson Miller, today made Jamaica's participation in the 13th FINA Junior Men's Water Polo World Championships a reality when she handed ASAJ President $2 million. This will go a long way in funding the $2.8 million budget.

September 9, 2005 -The Sport Development Foundation today continued it's support for Jamaica's aquatics programme when ASAJ President John Eyre was handed a check valued $300,000. The grant will used to send Jamaica's Junior Men's Water Polo Team to Argentina for the FINA World Championships.
