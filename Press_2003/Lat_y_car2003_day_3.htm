MORE MEDALS FOR THE JAMAICANS SWIMMING IN THE LATYCAR MASTERS COMPETITION

Home

September 5, 2003  
      
Wednesday saw all five Jamaicans in the pool with Lynval Lowe and Nigel Bair splashing into action in the 40-44 age group. The team swam in the 50 metre Butterfly, 100 metre Breastroke and 200 metre Backsroke events.
                 

In the 40-44 age group "Mr Fitness " Ricardo Santos took the butterfly  handily in 26.68 and Bruce Kaufmann of Trinidad and Tobago clocked 29.54 for the silver and deny Allan-Marsh a higher poduim placing with his 30.53. Marsh in turn prevented Lowe from a medal placing as he clocked 31.16 for fourth just off his season best 30.71 . Nigel Bair who has suffered from shoulder problems whichas prevented him from diving in practise recorded a 34.87 for 7th in his first competitive outing for the year.   

                

In the 45-49 age group Lee  in a very close race tied for the bronze in 31.27 with Richard Knaggs of Tand T's Trinity Masters silver went to Panama's Carlos Gonzales in 30.99 and gold to local Geoffrey Farmer in 30.11.

              

Lowe in the following event the 100 metre Breastroke had a close battle with Brazil's Sergio Ulloa up to 75 metres until the Brazilian pulled away utilizing a smoother stroke action which made him get more distance per stroke. In the end it was Ulloa from Lowe 1:18.65 to 1:20.91

                                 

It took the last event of the day for the team to land take the gold and yet it was to be in the Backstroke races. In the 40-44 age group it was the 1984 Olympian Allan -Roy who dismissed  the competition with a very smooth and controlled stroke action to take the event with seemingly minimal effort in 2:39.86 beating second place by almost ten seconds. In the 19-24 category "Tony" Morrison landed gold when he took the race by well over 25 metres over Mexican Luis Soriano Escammilla. Following the advice of his local club coach Michael Wilson to utilize the kicks fully he came home in a new season and personal best time by two seconds of 2:30.94

                

To at the end of day three Jamaica's medal tall stands at 6 Gold, 3 Silver and 2 Bronze.

Submitted by Tony Morrison.
