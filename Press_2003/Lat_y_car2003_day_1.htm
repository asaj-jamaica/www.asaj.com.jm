JAMAICANS MEDAL ON DAY 1 AT THE LATYCAR MASTERS SWIM MEET

Home

September 2, 2003  
 

          Monday saw three of the Jamaica's team in action with Michael Lee in the 200 I.M. and Allen -Roy Marsh and Anthony Morrison-Vincent in the 100 metre backstroke and at the end of the day all three had medalled. In the 2nd event of the meet, Michael Lee overcame a body length deficit in the fly leg to draw level with Geoffrey Farmer at the halfway mark, but a better pullout on the Breastroke and Freestyle was the difference between the men as Farmer won in 2:40.33 and Lee closing well in 2;43.87. Lee bettered his time by over 2 seconds pulling away from another fast finishing competitor in the 40-44 age group from Costa Rica .

           In the 40-44 100 metre Backstoke Allan Roy Marsh made the race his own from the get -go and by 25 metres the race was already over. So great was his advantage that the announcer mistakenly told the spectators that a new Latycar record had been established. Marsh finished in 1:09.73 beating his nearest competitor by over six seconds

          In the 19-24 age group Anthony Morrison-Vincent  had an easy victory dismissing his Mexican competitor by over 24 seconds to win in 1:07.51 a season best.

          At the end of Day one Jamaica's medal tally stands at two gold and a silver.
