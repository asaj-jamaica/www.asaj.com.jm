Jamaicans swim at Florida Senior Circuit meet in Naples

Home

January, 21, 2003

With no Jamaicans swimming, glory had to be gained on a regional basis through the efforts of our Caribbean colleagues from Barbados' Bradley Ally and Sean Dehere representing Fort Lauderdale swim team.
 

The presence of Venezuelan Olympian Richard Moasterio who is also the current  CAC champion and record holder in the 400 3:57.63 and 1500 15:39.90 did not prove daunting for the 16 year old phenom as he rose to the challenge of his quality competitor. In the 200 meter Butterfly, Ally gave the Venezuelan a huge scare. He led his more heralded rival at the half way mark 1:04.09 to 1:04.26 but lost it at the death when the CAC champ recovered to out split Ally, 1:07.60 to 1:08.31 to finish in a total time of 2:11.86 to Bradley's 2:12.40.

 
The Man with the broken trident then  set his sights on the 200 metre Breastroke and the 200 individual medley. In both events he took the measure of Eric Donnelly Pan American 400 Individual Medley silver medalist and a member of the US 2002 Pan Pacific and World Short Course Championship teams. In the shorter version the Individual Medley the 200 ( in which he was ranked 3rd for all club swimmers in the 15-16 age group in the States) he took the lead at the 100 metre mark 1:03.16 to 1:0.04 and though with a slower split for the second 100 1:09.61 to 1:08.95 he did enough to take the title 2:12.77 to 2:12.99.
 
In the 200 Breastroke he came from behind 1:15.86 1:15.79 at the halfway mark  to power past his American counterpart and win in 2:35.36 to his competitor's 2:36.77 to complete the double as he took the 100 in 1;10.28 earlier in the meet
         
Sean Dehere, a Carifta medal hopeful at this year's edition of the Championships here in Jamaica performed well also taking third in the 100 Freestyle in 56.50 and 2nd in the 50 freestyle, 25.34, being just pipped by Brett Irvine from Pine Crest who took the "splash and dash" in 25.32
