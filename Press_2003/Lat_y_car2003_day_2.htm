JAMAICANS ADD 3 GOLD AND A SILVER AT THE LATYCAR MASTERS SWIM MEET

Home

September 4, 2003  
      
The second day of the bi-annual Latycar Masters competition saw the same members of the Jamaican team in action in the 100 metre Butterfly and the 50 metre Backstroke. The Jamaican trio led yet another series of wins in the backstroke events with Michael Lee getting in on the gold rush.

 

Lee started adding to the the treasure chest by taking the top spot in the 45-49 age group 50 M Backstroke in a season best 32.30 seconds, overwhelming the silver medallist by over ten seconds. Allan-Roy Marsh made his contribution to the medal tally by recording a sizzling 31.85 seconds, another season best with a comfortable victory margin in the 40-45 age group. Youngster "Tony" Morrison further enhanced the backstroke reputation of the Jamaicans when he touched first to take the Gold in 31.03 just .01 off his season best.
  

In the 100 metre butterfly Marsh missed his season best by .05 to record 1:09.88 for the the  silver. The race was won 42 year old Brazilian Ricardo Santos dubbed "Mr Fitness" by the Jamaican team. He took the event in 59.22 and then came back to win the 200 freestyle in a very impressive 2:05.76. In both events he had the second fastest times for all age groups. Recent Pan -American competitor Cliiff Gittens took the butterfly event in a seemingly effortless 59.42
