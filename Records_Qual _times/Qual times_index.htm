NATIONAL SW TEAM QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

General

The selection process for National Swim Teams is divided into two phases; qualification and team selection. In the qualification process, the names of swimmers who have met the qualification requirement will be gathered. The final team will be made by a selection committee whose decision will be submitted to Amateur Swimming Association of Jamaica (ASAJ) council for ratification.  The names of swimmers selected for representation at the CAC, Commonwealth, Pan American and Olympic Games will be submitted to the Jamaica Olympic Association (JOA), who has final authority.

As the ASAJ may not have access to all meet results, it is important that Coaches and Managers of swimmers, who have qualified, ensure that the relevant information providing proof of qualification is submitted prior to the qualification deadline. The ASAJ will not be held accountable for information not in its possession.

The following guidelines will apply;

1.        All times must be verified.

2.        When using the US Time standard as a qualifying criteria, the appropriate course chart for the year in question will be used e.g. if the criteria is “AA” times, then either the 50 M long course or 25 Y short course may be used.

3.        When considering final team selection, times will be convert to appropriate course (pool length) equivalents using the Hy-Tek conversion formula.

4.        Unless otherwise stated in the Meet Summons, only the top two in each event will be selected.

Top of SW Section

CARIFTA  2015

1.        Swimmer must be a Jamaica Citizen and holder of a valid Jamaican Passport which expires no earlier than October 31, 2015 or have resided in Jamaica for 12 months immediately prior to CARIFTA 2015.

2.        Swimmer must be registered with the ASAJ at least three weeks before the start of the last local qualifying meet.

3.        Times must have been swum in a meet sanctioned by the ASAJ or other FINA recognized body.

4.     Selection of swimmers will be based upon times achieved during the period October 1, 2014 - February 23, 2015.

5.     Qualifying times are based on the current TUSS times standards:

6.     Swimmers must achieve two (2) “AA” time standards in 2 different events, distances 100 metres or longer.

7.     For the 15 -17 Age Group, swimmers may achieve two (2)  of the average "3rd place" times in the last 3 CARIFTA games  in  two (2) different 50 metre events. 

8.     For the 15 – 17 Age Group, swimmers may achievement one (1) “AA” time standard in any event of 100 metres or longer; and achieve one (1)  of the average "3rd place" times in the last 3 CARIFTA games  in  50 metres events.

9.    After meeting the requirements of points 6-8 above, swimmer must be ranked in 1st and 2nd place in the respective events that the qualification standards have been achieved.

10.  Subject to achieving the qualification requirements of points 6-8 above, consideration will be given to selecting those swimmers ranked in the top 3 in the respective events that the qualification standards have been achieved

11.  Consideration will be given for selecting individual swimmers to be part of a freestyle and medley relay team whose combined times, less a 1.5 second relay take off factor, would have achieved a bronze medal or better placing at the 2014 CARIFTA Swimming Championships as determined by Hy-Tek TM. For 11-12 and 13-14 swimmers, this does not include the 4 x 50 metre freestyle relay. For swimmers to be considered, they must achieve a minimum "AA" time in the respective stroke.

12. Swimmers achieving "AA" times in Short Course yards and Short Course metrres sanctioned competitions during the qualifying period will be eligible for selection. However if times are equal via Hy-Tek conversion, Long Course times will take preference.

13.  All swimmers must indicate availability for selection to represent Jamaica at CARIFTA 2015 by January 31, 2015.

14.  Swimmers being considered for selection must demonstrate fitness by being in full training and prepared to complete in CARIFTA 2015. The respective coaches of swimmers being considered for selection may be required to confirm swimmers fitness.

 Top of SW Section

CCCAN 2015

1.        Competitor must be a Jamaican citizen and holder of a valid Jamaican Password which expires no earlier than December 31, 2015

2.        Swimmer must be registered with the ASAJ a minimum of three weeks prior to the start of the last local qualifying meet.

3.        Times must have been swum in a meet sanctioned by the ASAJ or other FINA recognized body.

4.     Qualifying times are based on the current Long Course TUSS times standards

5.    Selection of swimmers will be based upon times achieved during the period December 1 2014 – May 15 2015.

6.    Swimmers must achieve one (1) “AAAA” times in distances 100 metres or longer during the qualifying period

7.    For 15 - 17 and 18 & over age groups, the qualifying period is extended to achieving the time standard 1 year prior to the meet.

8.     Swimmers may also achieve a qualification time equal to, or faster than the average “3rd place” times in the last 3 CCCAN competitions, in a minimum of two (2) 50 metres events.

9.     Swimmers may also qualify if they achieved an individual gold medal in the 2015 CARIFTA Swimming Championships

10.   Consideration will be given to selecting individual swimmers to be part of a freestyle and medley relay teams whose combined times less a 1.5 second take over factor which would have achieved a gold medal at the 2015 CARIFTA Swimming Championship as determined by Hy-Tek

11.   Swimmer must be ranked 1st and 2nd place in the events where the swimmer has achieved the qualifying standards.

12.   All swimmers must indicate availability for selection to represent Jamaica at CCCAN 2015 by April 30 2015

13.  Swimmers being considered for selection must demonstrate fitness by being in full training and prepared to compete in CCCAN 2015. The respective coaches of swimmers being considered for selection may be required to confirm the swimmers fitness.

D     TECHNICAL AND TACTICAL EVALUATION

The elements below will be evaluated by the members of the National Selection Committee

 

1.       Recognition of general advantage situations

a.       Responsiveness to advantage situations as they occur

b.      Understanding of 6-on-5 and 5-on-6 positions and general strategy

2.       Practical understanding of umbrella and various positions

3.       Ability to execute press defense

4.       Physical strength in water

5.       Ability to protect ball under pressure

6.       Decision-making under pressure

7.       Passing ability

a.       Accuracy

b.      Power

c.       Appropriate pass selection

8.       Shooting

a.       Accuracy

b.      Power

c.       Appropriate shot selection

9.       Discipline

10.   Level of motivation and effect on others
